My Story

My name is Bridget, and I’ve been writing about personal finance for nearly 5 years on Money After Graduation, which has featured in Business Insider, MoneySense, The Globe and Mail, Questrade, and more. I am passionate about helping 20-somethings and 30-somethings build wealth to fund their dreams. When I was a new investor, I struggled to find resources that were easy to understand and written for twenty-somethings. All the books out there were either overly-technical or geared towards investors in their 50’s and 60’s. After three years of DIY-investing in my portfolio with many wins and losses, I went back to school for an MBA, specializing in Finance.

In my MBA program I studied macro- and micro-economic trends that impact global stock markets. I learned how to use financial derivatives like stock options to mitigate investment risk. I analyzed balance sheet after balance sheet to identify companies that are good for investment, and those that are not. I was trained to manage a hedgefund, but now I want to bring a superior level of financial literacy to the masses. I want to teach millennials to invest — safely, profitably, and with confidence. The stock market is exciting. Investing is fun. But like any skill, it takes time to learn how to do it well. My MBA in Finance took tens of thousands of dollars and two years of full-time study to earn, but I want everyone to reap the benefits. My goal is to make millennials rich. Especially you.
